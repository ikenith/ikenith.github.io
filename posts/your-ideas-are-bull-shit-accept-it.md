What counts is the **number of attempts**, **number of times you took action and failed badly** and **not** the number of ideas you generated.

In a structured way, it should be written like this:

> **Think → Generate → Act**

Did you fail?  
Did you succeed?  
We will care about that later.

---

## What to take from this today? (A story about writings.)

**The answer:**

What you are thinking about—whether it is a product or an idea—can be the same as what another person out there is thinking.  
But the moment you **act on it**, you are already ahead of **50% of people**.

Research on writing practice shows that people who write consistently improve faster than those who only plan. In classroom and journaling action-research studies, regular writing practice increased writing speed by **around 67%** and total writing output by **about 80%**, simply through consistency and repetition.

**Source:**  
Enhancing Writing Fluency Through Journaling – [Action Research Study](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/393901746_ENHANCING_WRITING_FLUENCY_THROUGH_JOURNALING_A_CLASSROOM_ACTION_RESEARCH)

For a long time, I believed that before putting my thoughts out on my blog, I had to become a good writer—learn grammar, read hard books, and wait for my writing to magically improve.

Bull shit.

Write **100 words a day for one month**, daily, and you are already **six months ahead of yourself**.

This does **not** mean you don’t have to learn grammar or read hard books anymore. If you want to become the best, you *will* have to read—no doubt about that.

But to be a writer, what I believe is simple:

**Simple English is enough to speak with more than a Billion people.**
